Research Generative music ICCC 2020

Music Generation for Visual Art through Emotion

Generating music from images, using emotion as the bridge between the visual and auditory domains. Pre-trained image representations drive two music generation architectures, one RNN-based and one Transformer-based. Human and machine evaluations indicate both generate music that carries emotional resonance. By Xiaodong Tan and Mathis Antony, published at the International Conference on Computational Creativity 2020.

Installation Computer vision Text generation

I am {emotion}

An interactive installation: reclining chairs where visitors listen to machine-generated self-improvement advice. Computer vision analyses the participant's facial features, and a text model trained on self-help literature generates a mix of spiritual, motivational and self-help guidance shaped by the detected emotions. By Manolis Perrakis and Mathis Antony, with Xiaodong Tan (text generation) and Cristina Kountiou (textiles). Shown at Ars Electronica 2019 and Sónar Hong Kong 2019.

Deep learning Medical imaging 2nd prize

Diabetic Retinopathy Detection

2nd prize in the Kaggle Diabetic Retinopathy Detection competition. VGG-style convolutional neural networks on large colour retina images, with heavy data augmentation, dynamic resampling for class imbalance, and a per-patient feature blending strategy that combines predictions across both of a patient's eyes.

Demiurge

A generative audio project by Roberto Alonso Trillo and Marek Poliks. We worked with them on the technical implementation, building neural audio synthesis pipelines around descriptor transformers, MelGAN and UnaGAN.
